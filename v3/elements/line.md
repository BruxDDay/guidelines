         <td class="wovenodd-col1">
            <strong>Remarks</strong>
         </td>
         <td class="wovenodd-col2">
            <p>The starting point of the line may be identified in absolute output coordinate terms
               using the 
               <span class="att">x</span> and 
               <span class="att">y</span> attributes. The attributes in the att.visualoffset
               class may be used to record horizontal, vertical, or time offsets from these absolute
               coordinates or from the location of the element reference in the 
               <span class="att">startid</span>
               attribute. Similarly, the terminal point of the line may be recorded using the
               
               <span class="att">x2</span> and 
               <span class="att">y2</span> attributes. Attributes in the att.visualoffset2 class
               maybe used to record the offsets of the ending point. Textual content of the 
               <a class="link_odd_elementSpec" href="/{{ site.baseurl }}/{{ page.version }}/elements/line.html">line</a> element, e.g. 'gliss.', may be rendered with the line. The
               appearance of the line is captured in the color, 
               <span class="att">form</span> and 
               <span class="att">width</span>
               attributes.
            </p>
         </td>
      </tr>

         <td class="wovenodd-col2">
            <div>
               <div>When used in the symbolDef context, must have either a startid attribute or x and
                  y attributes.
               </div>
               <div>When used in the symbolDef context, must have either an endid attribute or both x2
                  and y2 attributes.
               </div>
               <div>When used in the score context, must have a startid, tstamp, tstamp.ges or tstamp.real
                  attribute or both x and y attributes.
               </div>
               <div>When used in the score context, must have an endid, dur, dur.ges, or tstamp2 attribute
                  or both x2 and y2 attributes.
               </div>
